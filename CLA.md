# Contributor License Agreement (CLA) — why and how

FlyMax is **open core**: this orchestrator is MIT-licensed and open, while a future
enterprise edition will be closed-source and commercial. That model only works
legally if every contribution to the open repo can also be used in the commercial
edition. A CLA makes that explicit and protects both you and the project.

## What a CLA does
- You keep the copyright to your contribution.
- You grant FlyMax a broad licence to use, relicense, and include your contribution
  in both the open and the commercial editions.
- You confirm the contribution is yours to give (not your employer's, not copied from
  incompatible code).

## Status
**This is a placeholder pending legal review.** Until a formal CLA is adopted, the
terms in `CONTRIBUTING.md` apply: *by contributing you agree your work is MIT-licensed.*
For an open-core project that intends to ship a commercial edition, a proper CLA
(individual + entity versions, e.g. via the Apache ICLA model or a CLA-assistant bot)
should be put in place before accepting substantial external contributions.
